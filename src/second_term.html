Analysis of Decisions

Decision-making with data, payoff tables, and clear rules such as Expected Monetary Value (EMV), Maximin, and Maximax.

Conceptual Referents

  • Random variable
  • Probability distributions
  • Subjective probability
  • Mean and variance of discrete distributions
  • Investment portfolio
  • Expected value

Achievement Criteria

  1. Describes the way a problem can be formulated for optimal decision making.
  2. Interprets the parts of a problem such as decision alternatives, fortuitous events, consequences, and states of nature.
  3. Builds a payoff table from a description of the problem.
  4. Explains how decision-making without probabilities works (criteria: maximax and maximin).
  5. Develops decision-making strategies to address problems with probabilities using EMV.
  6. Compares EMV, maximin, and maximax and explains when their recommendations diverge.
  7. Evaluates all possibilities in a decision-making problem using the different strategies presented so far.

Decision Process

  • Define alternatives: List feasible actions.
  • Identify states: Outcomes of nature that affect payoffs.
  • Assign probabilities: Based on data or expert judgment.
  • Evaluate: Compute expected values or utilities, then choose.

Decision Rules: EMV, Maximin, and Maximax

Use a payoff table to compare options across the same states of nature. EMV multiplies each payoff by its probability and adds the results. Maximin focuses on the strongest worst-case payoff, while Maximax focuses on the highest possible payoff.

Example with diverging recommendations: A firm has three technology strategies under three demand scenarios (payoffs in $000s).
State of Nature Probability Innovate (A) Improve (B) License (C)
High demand 0.30 150 110 70
Moderate demand 0.40 80 90 70
Low demand 0.30 -20 40 60
EMV calculations: \(\text{EMV}_A = (0.30 \times 150) + (0.40 \times 80) + (0.30 \times -20) = 71\text{k}\); \(\text{EMV}_B = (0.30 \times 110) + (0.40 \times 90) + (0.30 \times 40) = 81\text{k}\); \(\text{EMV}_C = (0.30 \times 70) + (0.40 \times 70) + (0.30 \times 60) = 67\text{k}\). EMV recommends Improve (B). Maximin: Worst payoffs are A = -20k, B = 40k, C = 60k. The highest worst case is 60k, so Maximin recommends License (C) for maximum safety. Maximax: Best payoffs are A = 150k, B = 110k, C = 70k. The largest best case is 150k, so Maximax recommends Innovate (A). Because the three rules weigh risk differently, they point to three distinct choices even though they use the same payoff table.
