Analysis of Decisions

Structure choices under uncertainty using payoff tables, EMV, and utilities.

Decision Process

  • Define alternatives: List feasible actions.
  • Identify states: Outcomes of nature that affect payoffs.
  • Assign probabilities: Based on data or expert judgment.
  • Evaluate: Compute expected values or utilities, then choose.

Decision Trees & EMV

Decision trees show decisions (squares) and chance nodes (circles). The expected monetary value (EMV) at a chance node is the probability-weighted average of payoffs.

Example: Choose between a safe project ($40k guaranteed) and a risky project (40% chance of $120k, 60% chance of $10k). EMV(risky) = 0.4×120k + 0.6×10k = $54k > $40k, so risky dominates by EMV.

Risk & Utility

  • Risk-neutral: Maximize EMV.
  • Risk-averse: Use concave utility (e.g., U(x)=√x) to value outcomes.
  • Risk-seeking: Use convex utility to emphasize large gains.

Sensitivity analysis tests how decisions change when probabilities or payoffs shift.
